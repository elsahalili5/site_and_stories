REGION: FERIZAJ

MUNICIPALITY : SHTIME

SITE: REÇAK

DATE:15 JANUARY 1999

On January 15, 1999, three months after Serbia had pledged to the international community (Holbrooke-Milosevic Agreement of October 1998) that it would not take any action against the Albanian population in Kosovo, Serbia killed and maimed while alive 38 Albanian civilians in Reçak/Recak, Municipality of Shtime/Stimlje. This action by Serbia was qualified as such by both the EU and the US in a joint statement. Serbian Police, assisted by a motorized military unit, surround the village at night, and on the morning of January 15, shelling begins from four different positions (from sites known as “Pishat”, “Gështenjat”, “Rrasa e Çallakut” and from the position known as “Çesta”). Around noon, Serbian Police forces advance into the village on foot, entering each house. Overwhelmed by fear, some villagers take refuge in the basement and farm of one of the villagers named Sadik Osman. About 40 Serbian Police manage to enter Osman’s farm and force out about 30 men and boys from the village, first they ask for and confiscate all documents, then proceed to brutally beat them with wooden sticks and metal batons, ordering them to walk in the direction of hill (site known as “Gropa e Bebushit”), where they start firing automatic weapons in their direction, 24 out of 30 men are killed in this place, while Ramë Shabani, Bilall Bilalli, Nesret Shabani, Ali Qazimi, and Imer Imeri, residents of Reçak/Recak village, covered by the killed bodies manage to make it out alive. On that same day, in other parts of the village of Reçak/Recak, Serbian forces killed 14 other people, some had been struck on the head with axes, three of them minors. The representative of the international civilian mission to Kosovo at that time, William Walker, upon visiting the scene after the withdrawal of Serbian forces, declared and qualified this act as a crime against humanity.
