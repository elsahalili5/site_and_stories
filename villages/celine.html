REGION:GJAKOVË

MUNICIPALITY: RAHOVEC

SITE: CELINË

DATE:26 MARCH 1999

On March 26, 1999, combined Serbian police and military forces surrounded the village of Celinë/Celina in the Municipality of Rahovec/Orahovac and began to enter Albanian houses, kicking out dozens of residents of this settlement, executing 76 of them, mutilating several persons while alive, among them 10 children, one barely 18 months old. The terror on the civilian population lasted for two whole days and the victims remained unburied for several days in their courtyards, basements, houses, and other spaces. One of the members of the Serbian Police has appeared before the International Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia in The Hague, as a protected witness (K-41) providing testimony, inter alia, that they had a firm order to carry out a massacre of the Albanian civilian population in retaliation for the NATO bombing of the former Yugoslavia.
