REGION: GJAKOVË

MUNICIPALITY:RAHOVEC

SITE:BELLACËRKË

DATE:25 MARCH 1999

On March 25, 1999, one day after the start of the NATO air campaign against Serbian military and police targets, Serbian military units took positions in the village of Bellacërkë/Bela Crkva. Residents from all over the village had begun to gather near the river. They went to a place knows as Ura e Bellasë (Bridge of Bela). About 800 villagers attempted to hide under the bridge. Around 9:30 a.m., 16 special unit police officers opened fire with automatic weapons. There were two families that had separated from the group, Serbian Police fired upon them, killing all but a 2-year-old boy, who made it out alive because his mother had shielded him with her body. The first information about the scale of this crime was initially covered by the New York Times. Further, other residents were stopped at the railway platform. The men were separated from the women and children, then they ordered the men to undress. Serbian forces took all their belongings, then ordered the women and children to go in the direction of Xërxë/Zrze. The men were ordered to go to the riverbank, where they were executed and some were mutilated with axes and knives, among them five children.
