REGION:GJAKOVË

MUNICIPALITY:GJAKOVË

SITE: MEJË

DATE:27 APRIL 1999

The village of Mejë/Meja in Gjakovë/Djakovica is duly considered and referred to as Kosovo’s Srebrenica. There, Serbian police, military, and units engaged by Serbia, within a few hours, killed all the boys and men captured alive in the 15 villages around Mejë/Meja. In the morning of April 27, 1999, units engaged by Serbia surrounded from all sides the area known as “Reka e Keqe” (Bad River) and entered every Albanian house, forcibly removing all boys and men over the age of 15 years old from their homes and sending them away to the village of Mejë/Meja. Within a few hours, 284 people were killed and mutilated by axes by the Serbian Police. A few weeks later, Serbian forces returned to the site; in an attempt to cover up the crime, some bodies were exhumed, and they have been missing ever since. Some bodies have been found in mass graves in Serbia.
