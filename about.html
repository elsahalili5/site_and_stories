SITES AND STORIES IS AN ATTEMPT TO PRESERVE AND PRESENT WAR CRIMES IN KOSOVO AS AN IMPORTANT HERITAGE OF OUR COUNTRY

Kosovo was an integral part of former Yugoslav federation. 92% of the 2 million population in Kosovo are Albanians. They felt politically discriminated in the Yugoslav system. In 1989, Slobodan Milosevic comes to power as the leader of Serbia and ordered constitutional changes at the level of the Yugoslav federation, which were rejected by almost all Yugoslav republics and provinces.

In response to the emerging dissatisfaction, Serbia initially attacks Slovenia and later manipulates and mobilizes local Serbs in Croatia and Bosnia and Herzegovina, who began fighting from 1991 to 1995, with an estimated 200,000 civilians killed. In Kosovo, on the other hand, Serbia abolishes the autonomy that Kosovo had gained in 1974.

In early 1998, police forces and units engaged by Serbia began mass killings in the Drenica region, killing 24 Albanian civilians in the villages of Qirez and Likoshan on 27 February (1998), including a child and a pregnant woman.

From February 1998 to June 1999, an estimated 402 massacres were committed by Serbia against the Albanian civilian population in Kosovo, with over 13,000 people killed.
